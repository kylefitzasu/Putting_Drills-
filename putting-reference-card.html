The Putting Card

Mallet · Straight Back Straight Through · Shoulder-Driven
Setup
◆
Stance — shoulder-width, square to line
◆
Ball position — just forward of center (left of sternum)
◆
Eyes — over ball or just inside the line
◆
Weight — 60/40 lead side, stays there
◆
Grip — 4/10 pressure, forearms lightly connected
◆
Spine — forward tilt from hips, stays constant
Mental Sequence
1 Read from behind the hole
2 Pick spot 6" in front of ball
3 Set up to the spot, not the hole
4 One practice stroke for distance
5 Look at hole once → back to ball
6 Stroke within 3 seconds
The Stroke
1
Takeaway — left shoulder tilts down, right up. No wrist hinge. Putter stays low.
2
Rock, don't rotate — seesaw motion along spine. Stroke length = distance control.
3
Transition — gravity starts forward stroke. Right shoulder tilts down smoothly.
4
Impact — hands even with ball. Slight ascending strike (1–2°). Face square.
5
Follow through — equal to or longer than backswing. Hold finish. Face to target.
Miss Diagnosis
5 Feet
↪ Push Right
Hands/arms take over, steering the face open
→ Soften grip · Gate drill · Trust the rock
10 Feet
↩ Pull Left
Shoulders rotating instead of rocking — path cuts left
→ Seesaw cue · Alignment stick drill
Pre-Round Checklist
Ball position correct?
Controls push/pull
Shoulders rocking, not rotating?
Controls 10-foot pull
Follow through ≥ backswing?
Controls deceleration
Grip pressure soft (4/10)?
Quiets hands, stops push
